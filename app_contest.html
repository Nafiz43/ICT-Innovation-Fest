Mobile App Contest

  • Deadline: 30th September, 2021
  • Eligible Candidates: University Students
  • Overview
  • Are you a dreamer? Do you constantly think about new and innovative ways to do something more productively? Want to make your creative ideas come to life with an app? The 'Creative app contest' segment of the 'Online Innovation Fest' hosted by CSE Department, MIST is giving you the opportunity to shine. Participate in the contest, amaze everyone with your coding skill and innovation; and take home the prize money and certificate.
    Three prizes from each category, i.e., a total of nine prizes will be given for the Mobile App Contest. Prize money for each category:
    First prize: 20,000
    Second prize: 15,000
    Third prize: 10,000
    Focus of the mobile applications: For this contest, we are looking for the android apps that belong to any of the three main categories, that includes: (a) Education, (b) Bangabandhu and 50 years of Bangladesh , and (c) Humanitarian Apps. The details are given as follows.

Education

  • Innovative mobile apps for online education platforms that can bridge the gap between teachers and students, or in certain situations, parents. For example, Apps for Remote learning and education management; Apps for imparting education such as: mobile games, special apps for students with disabilities, IoT devices, social/digital platforms, etc. Again, during the Covid-19 pandemic, with the goal of alleviating the global crisis regarding online education, specific innovative apps for students and teachers are very much needed. Thus, mobile applications for mitigating the crisis of online education during the time of COVID-19 crisis could be another potential focus of this category.

Bangabandhu and 50 years of Bangladesh Category

  • The father of the Nation Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman had a vision of Shonar Bangla where the people of his country will live without despair. Thus, we are looking for innovative Apps that focus on celebrating the father of the nation and the 50 years of our independence by digitizing national history, national achievements and goals. This category also includes, but not limited to the apps the Apps that are educational in nature and convey the rich history and heritage of the country. Again, in this category you may foccus to identifying a national crisis that is hindering our progress to digitization to form the Digital Bangladesh and an innovative solution to overcome such problems.

Humanitarian Services

  • Apps with the intention to provide humanitarian related information from emergencies around the world such as COVID-19 relief and information, disaster assessments, medical aid or food relief during or after this pandemic, blood banks, donating excess foods, clothes, medics, as well as providing fastest route and weather condition to overcome the limited resources are to be included in this category.

Submission Guidelines

  1. Compile the following files/documents in a zip file. Rename the zip file with your team name. Submit the zip file as part of the registration process in the google form
  2. apk file: A complete runnable version of your app in apk format.
  3. Video: A video file explaining all the features of the application. Navigating through each page and audio visual information on the feature should be covered in the video. Video length will be maximum 3 mins.
  4. Document: The document should contain a synopsys that will highlight the objectives, key features, practical uses and implementation summary. The document length should not be more than 400 words.

Timeline

  • Problem Statement disclosure: September 1, 2021 (1 month before event)
  • Round 1 Submission: September 10, 2021 (20 days to event)
  • Shortlisted Team List publish: September 17, 2021 (13 days to event)
  • Round 2 (Final Round) Submission: September 28, 2021 (2 days to event)
  • Final Presentation: October 1, 2021 (Event Day)

    • Judgment Criteria

    1. Content: Text, music, sound, and animation -information that is provided in the app- all work together to communicate with the user in a clear and concise manner. Relevant, entertaining, and user-acceptable material is expected.
    2. Structure and Navigation: The framework of an application, the organizing of material, the priority of information, and the technique by which you travel across the app should be uniform, intuitive, and transparent.
    3. Visual Design: High-quality, user-friendly appearance and relevant for the target audience.
    4. Functionality/Performance:The app should perform properly, with all functionality implemented, and load efficiently, putting the app experience first and the technology second.
